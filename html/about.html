About

Find out about the latest reporting on the polar world in the pages of the New York Times.

The polar regions have attracted the attention of public audiences for centuries. Long after indigenous peoples had populated the circumpolar north, commerce, religion, science, and geopolitics led societies from North America, Europe and other parts towards the Arctic.

Today, the Arctic and the Antarctic are most often associated with the dramatic effects of the global climate emergency. As sea water rise and glaciers continue to receede at an ever-faster pace, the Arctic is warming four times as fast as the rest of the planet.

The Icy News web application offers a narrow but deep glimpse into the world of New York Times journalism about the polar regions. The website enables professional and non-professional researchers to locate information about the peoples and the environments at the ends of the world.
